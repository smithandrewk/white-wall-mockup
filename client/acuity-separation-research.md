# Acuity Scheduling & Squarespace: Separation Research

## TL;DR
**Acuity is a separate subscription from Squarespace.** Canceling Squarespace does NOT cancel Acuity. No data migration needed — just embed Acuity on the new site and cancel Squarespace.

---

## Key Findings

### Acuity is NOT bundled with Squarespace
- Squarespace acquired Acuity in 2019, but they remain **separate paid subscriptions**
- Acuity is an optional add-on, not included free with any Squarespace plan
- The pricing is the same whether you sign up through Squarespace or directly at acuityscheduling.com

### Login Situation
- Squarespace has been **unifying login systems** — legacy Acuity accounts now log in through Squarespace's auth
- If Drew says "I don't know the Acuity login" — **it's their Squarespace login**
- A Squarespace account is auto-created for Acuity users who didn't have one

### What Happens When You Cancel Squarespace
- **Cancel Squarespace website only → Acuity stays active.** Clients can still book. No data loss.
- Cancel Acuity subscription (but don't delete) → Account becomes inactive, data preserved, can reactivate
- **Delete Acuity subscription → All data permanently lost and unrecoverable**
- ⚠️ **NEVER delete the Acuity subscription. Only cancel the Squarespace website.**

### Embedding on External Sites
Acuity provides embed options for ANY website (not just Squarespace):
1. **Full scheduler embed** — iframe embed code, paste into any HTML page
2. **Booking button** — link/button that opens scheduling page
3. **Booking bar** — persistent bar overlay

Embed code is available in Acuity dashboard: **Acuity > Share Your Scheduling Page > Embed Code**

### Before Canceling Squarespace (Safety Exports)
1. Export client list: Clients > Import/Export > Export client list
2. Export appointment history: Reports > Export (select date range)
3. Screenshot all appointment types, availability settings, and intake forms (just in case)
